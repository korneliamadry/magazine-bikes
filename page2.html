______ What to look for when buying a bike? ______

  • in general: check if it's free of rust and dents, if you like the design and also choose the type of bike which will be suitable for your use (road/ off road/ tricks // weekly, everyday use)
  • frame: size, so your knees don't touch to the steering wheel (bad-chosen may frustrate)
  • wheels: if centered and in good quality
  • chain: fluidity; it should move smoothly
  • brake: condition in action and also a type - disc brakes are more complicated in construction, difficult to repair on your own and they are heavier but they react faster than jaw brakes and are more efficiency in rain. Avoid V-brakes made of plastic.
  • saddle: comfortable, contoured
  • wheel hoops: reinforced, aluminium, bigger to the city 28" and smaller ones 26" in the field
  • gears: standard is 3 gears at the front and 7 in the back; 6 gears are the vintage construction
  • shock absorbers: two heavy are not recommended on asphalt driving
  • the bike should not be too heavy, ideally within 15kg
  • "try a bike" before buying, than you'll immediately feel if it's comfortable

    • Some things can be replaced after, if the price of buying is still profitable.

It's worth looking around for bikes and accessories in shops in January, because then there are the biggest discounts.

______ Additional indicated equipment ______

Helmet, lamps (white and red), fender, basket, small pump, additional tube or at least patch sticker or tape, multi tools - in the event of a breakdown.
